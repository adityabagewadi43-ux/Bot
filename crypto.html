🪙 What is Cryptocurrency?

Cryptocurrency is a digital form of money that operates independently of central banks. It uses cryptographic techniques to secure transactions and control the creation of new units. Unlike traditional currencies, cryptocurrencies are decentralized and run on blockchain networks.

The most popular cryptocurrency is Bitcoin (BTC), introduced in 2009. Since then, thousands of cryptocurrencies have been developed, each with unique features and use cases.

📖 Extended Crypto Knowledge

What Is A Blockchain?

A blockchain is a public, tamper‑proof ledger where every transaction is recorded in blocks, linked together in chronological order. Each block contains a cryptographic hash of the previous block, making it very hard to alter past data.

Proof of Work vs Proof of Stake

Proof of Work (PoW) uses miners who solve complex math puzzles to secure the network and create new blocks (e.g., Bitcoin).

Proof of Stake (PoS) selects validators based on how much crypto they “stake” as collateral (e.g., Ethereum after The Merge).

Wallets: Hot vs Cold

Hot wallets are connected to the internet (apps, exchanges, browser wallets). They are convenient but more exposed to hacks.

Cold wallets are offline devices or hardware wallets (like USB‑style devices). They are slower to use but much safer for long‑term storage.

How To Buy Crypto (Simple Steps)

  • Choose a trusted crypto exchange (e.g., Binance, Coinbase, Kraken).
  • Verify your identity and enable 2‑factor authentication (2FA).
  • Fund your account with fiat (INR, USD, etc.) via bank transfer, card, or UPI.
  • Buy BTC, ETH, or other coins and move them to your personal wallet.

Trading vs Investing

Investing (often “HODLing”) means buying crypto and holding it long‑term, expecting value to grow over time.

Trading means actively buying and selling to catch short‑term price swings (day trading, swing trading, scalping).

Key Risks & Security Tips

  • Crypto is highly volatile; prices can drop 30–50% in a short time.
  • Use strong passwords and 2FA on all accounts.
  • Never share your private keys or seed phrases.
  • Avoid “too good to be true” yield or giveaway scams.
