🪙 What is Cryptocurrency?

Cryptocurrency is a digital form of money operating independently of central banks. It uses cryptographic techniques to secure transactions and control new unit creation — running on decentralized blockchain networks.

The most popular is Bitcoin (BTC), introduced in 2009. Thousands of cryptos have since emerged with unique features and use cases.

⚙️ How Cryptocurrency Works

Transactions are broadcast to a peer-to-peer network, verified by miners or validators using Proof of Work or Proof of Stake, then permanently etched onto the immutable blockchain ledger.

📖 Extended Crypto Knowledge

What Is A Blockchain?

A public tamper-proof ledger where every transaction is recorded in chronologically linked blocks. Each block contains a cryptographic hash of the previous one — making past data nearly impossible to alter.

Proof of Work vs Proof of Stake

PoW — Miners solve complex math puzzles to add blocks and earn rewards (Bitcoin).

PoS — Validators stake coins as collateral to propose and validate new blocks (Ethereum post-Merge).

Wallets: Hot vs Cold

Hot wallets — internet-connected (apps, exchanges). Convenient but more vulnerable to attacks.

Cold wallets — offline hardware devices. Slower to access but far safer for long-term storage.

How To Buy Crypto

  • Choose a trusted exchange (Binance, Coinbase, CoinDCX).
  • Verify identity and enable 2-factor authentication.
  • Fund via bank / UPI, then buy your chosen coin.
  • Transfer to a personal wallet for maximum security.

Trading vs Investing

Investing (HODLing) — buy and hold long-term expecting value to grow over time.

Trading — actively buy/sell to profit from short-term price swings (day/swing/scalping).

Key Security Tips

  • Prices can drop 30–50% rapidly — size positions conservatively.
  • Use strong unique passwords + 2FA on every platform.
  • Never share your seed phrase or private keys with anyone.
  • Avoid "guaranteed returns" or giveaway scams at all costs.
